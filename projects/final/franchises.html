Mario

A game series featuring Nintendo's Mario. The founder is Shigeru Miyamoto. Nintendo's flagship franchise and mascot, which has received critical acclaim and recognition worldwide, has been the most sold video game series of all. As it is a game series that has had the biggest impact on the development of video games, many people regard it as a symbol of modern video games beyond a symbol of Nintendo. The original series is a Super Mario series, but the popularity of the character Mario has increased, and variations such as the Mario Kart series and the Mario Party series have since emerged. Those series are also as popular as the original series, so the Mario series is one of the few franchises that both the main series and the spin-off series have hit jackpot.

Representative games: Super Mario Series, Yoshi Series, Mario Kart Series, Mario Party Series, and a total of 250 other games.

Kirby

The "Kirby" series is an action game series developed by HAL Laboratory and released by Nintendo in 1992 by "Kirby." The game features children's game-like appearance with a peaceful atmosphere, mostly set in Planet Popstar and Dream Land, as well as lively, exciting and addictive BGM and cute characters. In particular, Kirby, the main character, is a soft pink ball-like creature and is very popular for its simple yet attractive character. However, the perception that it is simply a children's game is close to preconceived notions. In fact, many of the fans of the Kirby series of stars are attracted to the unique progression of the game itself. First of all, hovering, the ability to absorb air and inflate the body and float in the air, is the basic movement in most series. Thanks to this, there are relatively few restrictions on movement even though it is a horizontal scroll game. It is also possible to fly and avoid all enemies without having to kill them while passing by, or to fly through lava zones if you have good control. Kirby's various copying skills, such as his symbol, are also very different from other action games.

Representative games: Kirby's Dream Land, Kirby's Adventure, Kirby Super Star, Kirby & The Amazing Mirror, Kirby: Squeak Squad, Kirby Star Allies, Kirby and the Forgotten Land

Super Smash Bros.

It is a series of fighting action games developed by Masahiro Sakurai and released by Nintendo, and is a crossover fighting action game in which famous characters from Nintendo gather together to fight. Because of this game series, GameCube Controllers are still being released for generations. In addition to simple manipulation, unusual stages, and characters familiar with low barriers to entry due to items, it is easy for light users to enjoy, and it also adopts a number of deep systems as a full-fledged fighting game, so hard users can be satisfied. There are many different factors from ordinary fighting games because it is a game designed on a competitive basis called Antitezera. First of all, the winning condition of the battle fighting game, "Make the opponent's HP zero," is not works. The only way to KO the opponent is to fly the opponent out of the screen. Fighters have no physical strength and there is a system called "accumulative damage." Cumulative damage starts at 0% and accumulates more and more damage, and the longer the distance to attack increases.

Representative works: Super Smash Bros., Super Smash Bros. Melee, Super Smash Bros. Brawl, Super Smash Bros. for, Super Smash Bros. ULTIMATE.
