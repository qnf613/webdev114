1889~1970s: Origin as a card company

Founded as Nintendo Karuta on 23 September 1889 by craftsman Fusajiro Yamauchi in Shimogyō-ku, Kyoto, Japan, to produce and distribute hanafuda (花札, "flower cards"), a type of traditional Japanese playing card.

1977: Transformed into a video game company.

The company change to electronic toys, or video game machines business, which were high-tech at the time. Nintendo, which switched to the electronic game console business, established the Development Information Department in 1977 with Gunpei Yokoi as the leader.

Nintendo's first portable game machine, Game & Watch, was a hit in Japan, and it was also popular because it created an arcade game titled "Radar Scope."

At this time, a young industrial designer who graduated from college joined the company through his father's connections at a time when companies were reluctant to hire new employees due to the oil shock, and it was Shigeru Miyamoto, who made today's Nintendo.

1980: Entered the U.S. market.

Nintendo established Nintendo of America, its first overseas branch, as its first president, Arakawa Minoru, the son-in-law of President Hiroshi Yamauchi. However, Nintendo of America has failed to become popular and is on the verge of bankruptcy.

1981: The Donkey Kong

Original Nintendo urgently sent a slightly modified version of the Popeye Arcade, which Shigeru Miyamoto had developed but had not yet been released due to license issues, to Nintendo of America, and this game was the famous Donkey Kong, the debuet of Mario and Donkey Kong.
